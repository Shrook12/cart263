PROJECT TWO RESPONSE

The first project I am going to talk about is the 3D snake created by Ya Xuan, Ashmitha, and  Nerly. I like the idea of transforming the classic snake game into 3D and using sound to create more targets. You can even move the camera around to play from the view you prefer, or even try a different perspective, since some angles might be harder than others. Having more targets when there is more sound is really surprising; it makes you want to keep making any kind of sound, since when the sound stops, the targets disappear too. I also like the background, it gives a really nice touch to the game atmosphere! It’s also helpful that there are instructions at the beginning to explain how to play. For the code, I am impressed by the part they wrote to make the camera move around when you drag it. I thought they were using orbit controls, but they actually did it using mouse down and mouse move events. I didn’t know that there was another way to move the view. What I also like is that many things that we learned in class during the semester were incorporated into the code, like three.js, the click event, the microphone, the class and constructor, the key events, and the animation.

Link: https://yaxuanpang.github.io/cart263/topics/project2/


For the second project, I chose “Can You See Me?”, created by Yelena and Scarlett. The visuals in this project are AMAZING! The background, the atmosphere, the little forest, the trees, and the lights, everything works so well together and gives a Halloween vibe. I also like the background sounds, which blend perfectly with the atmosphere. I like how their JavaScript files are separated into three. One player.js where they have loaded the 3d model of the player. A game.js file, where I think they put all the conditions for winning or losing. Then they have a main.js, where they have all the code about the canvas, scene, renderer, music, orbit control, light, the keys to move the player, the start state, and the end state. It’s good for organization, but I also think it would be helpful to have like a comment at the top explaining what each page is doing, since the names for “main” and “game” can be a bit confusing.


Link: https://scarlett253.github.io/263_p2_ys/

For the third project, I am going to talk about Click The Cube by Jason and Haolei. I really like the design. It’s somewhat simple, and when you click, things change, and a different image or shape appears each time. I especially like what happens after 30 clicks on the cube: many cubes appear with a big”YOU ARE BREAKING THE SYSTEM” message, and then it goes away, and stars appear, and keep appearing every time you click. I also like how it’s not the same order of images that appear every time you refresh. The interaction that happens when you click on the cube, with little colored circles that appear. In the code, I saw that it has touch support for mobile devices, which is really cool because it means that this project works on phones as well! When I was using the app, I thought that the images was fetched from another site using API, like the example we did in class with the cat images, but looking at the code, it’s an array of images and they use random to show it in different orders. Now I am just curious about how they choose those images. Do they have a certain theme, or were they picked randomly?
